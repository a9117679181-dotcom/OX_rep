# Nigeria local counsel briefing - OX Network

Status: briefing pack for Nigerian gaming / fintech / tax / data counsel. This is not a legal opinion.

Date: 2026-06-11  
Project: OX Network / SuperExpress 15  
Current internal position: **RESEARCH MORE / possible CONDITIONAL GO only after Nigerian counsel and, where needed, regulator confirmation**.

## 1. Executive summary

OX Network is assessing whether SuperExpress 15 can be offered in Nigeria through a locally licensed operator. OX does not intend to accept Nigerian players directly. The proposed structure is B2B/B2B2C: the Nigerian operator provides the player relationship, local licence framework, marketing, support and local compliance; OX provides the technology, event selection, rules, smart contract, payout engine, audit layer and global/shared pool.

Nigeria is **not a clean GO** for a true global pool + USDC/Base + smart-contract settlement model. Current research flags critical unresolved issues around post-2024 federal/state gaming regulation, whether a local operator's licence can cover OX-controlled functions, whether OX needs direct supplier/software/platform/remote-operator approval, whether Nigerian players may join a cross-border pooled jackpot, and whether USDC tickets/payouts are permissible.

The conservative working path is **RESEARCH MORE**. A later **CONDITIONAL GO** may be possible only if counsel confirms a valid state/federal launch route, product/software approval, B2B supplier status or required OX approval, payment/crypto treatment, tax position, data-transfer basis, advertising approval process and liability allocation.

Several findings in the internal memo are intentionally classified as **UNCLEAR** or **UNKNOWN** because primary official materials were incomplete. Gaming-law analysis currently relies partly on secondary legal guides and law-firm alerts. Counsel should replace or confirm these with official statutes, regulator guidance, licence conditions and, where necessary, direct regulator engagement.

## 2. OX model summary

- **ASSUMPTION:** OX does not accept players directly in regulated countries, including Nigeria.
- **ASSUMPTION:** OX works through a locally licensed operator.
- **ASSUMPTION:** the local operator provides players, licence coverage, local marketing, support, player-facing terms and player relationship.
- **ASSUMPTION:** OX provides technology, event selection, smart contract, payout engine, audit, settlement logic and global/shared pool infrastructure.
- **Core legal question:** can OX remain a supplier/software provider, or would Nigerian regulators treat OX as a co-operator, pool/jackpot operator, remote operator, technical service provider, VASP/DAX/DAC, payment actor or another directly regulated participant?

## 4. Launch structures to evaluate

### Structure A: local operator + Nigeria-only segregated pool + local approved payment rails

Risk: **lower**, but still **UNCLEAR** until state/federal licence path and product approval are confirmed.

Question: can this be approved as an external B2B/B2B2C product under the local operator's licence, with OX disclosed as software/technology provider?

### Structure B: local operator + Nigeria sub-pool + mirrored/network jackpot display

Risk: **medium/high**.

Question: can a global or network jackpot be advertised without misleading players if Nigerian funds, payout liability and player participation are ring-fenced in a Nigeria/operator/licence sub-pool?

Counsel should also confirm whether display must distinguish:

- actual Nigerian pool;
- network/global marketing jackpot;
- seeded promotional amount;
- foreign-player-funded jackpot.

### Structure C: local operator + true global pool + USDC/Base + smart-contract settlement

Risk: **critical**.

Question: can Nigerian players legally participate in a cross-border pooled jackpot with USDC settlement on Base, where OX controls smart contract, payout rules and global liquidity?

This structure should not be treated as viable unless counsel and relevant regulators confirm gaming, fintech/VASP, banking/payment, tax, advertising and data protection treatment.
