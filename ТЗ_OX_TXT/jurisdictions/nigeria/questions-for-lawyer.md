# Nigeria - questions for local lawyer

Status: questionnaire for Nigerian gaming/fintech/data counsel. This is not a legal opinion.

Project: OX Network / SuperExpress 15  
Research date: 2026-06-11

## Product frame to confirm

- ASSUMPTION: OX does not accept players directly in regulated countries.
- ASSUMPTION: OX works as B2B/B2B2C infrastructure through locally licensed operators.
- ASSUMPTION: the local operator provides players, local licence framework, local marketing, support and player relationship.
- ASSUMPTION: OX provides technology, global/shared pool, smart contract, event selection, payout calculation, audit and operational support.

Core question: can OX be treated as B2B supplier/software/platform provider, or would Nigerian regulators treat OX as gambling co-operator, jackpot/pool operator, remote operator, technical service provider, VASP/payment actor or another directly regulated participant?

## Quality-audit priority questions

These questions are mandatory because the current research package has source gaps and may be incomplete after the 2024 Supreme Court decision on the National Lottery Act.

1. Please provide the official judgment or verified report for Suit No. SC/1/2008 and confirm its current legal effect.
2. After the Supreme Court decision, what is the exact current role of the NLRC outside the FCT?
3. Is any federal Remote Operator Permit still relevant outside the FCT, or must OX/local operator proceed state-by-state?
4. Are existing federal licences grandfathered, transitional or ineffective outside the FCT?
5. Which state regulator would govern launch if the first partner is incorporated in one state but accepts users from other Nigerian states?
6. Does the state-level answer differ for sports betting, pools betting, lotteries, totalisators and jackpot products?
7. Does any pending or proposed Central Gaming Bill or similar federal initiative affect launch timing or legal risk?
8. Please identify all official statutes, regulations, circulars, licence conditions and regulator guidance that supersede or qualify ICLG-style secondary summaries.
9. Please separate obligations for the B2C licensed operator from obligations for OX as B2B supplier, pool controller, settlement operator, VASP/payment actor and data processor/controller.
10. Please flag any conclusion below that cannot be answered without direct regulator engagement.
