# Nigeria - sources for OX Network legal research

Status: research source log. This is not a legal opinion.

Research date: 2026-06-11

## Source reliability key

- Primary: official regulator, government portal, statute/gazette, official regulatory document.
- Secondary: major legal guide or law firm material.
- Supplementary: news or market source. Not used as primary basis for conclusions.

## Core sources used

### NGA-GAM-001 - ICLG, Nigeria Gambling Laws and Regulations 2025

- Type: Secondary legal guide, authored by Nigerian lawyers at The Trusted Advisors.
- URL: https://iclg.com/practice-areas/gambling-laws-and-regulations/nigeria
- Relevance: gaming product classification, federal/state regulators, available licences/permits, online gambling, Remote Operator Permit, advertising, AML, taxes, software architecture requirements.
- Key evidence:
  - NLRC and state lottery boards regulate gaming, betting and lotteries in online/land-based forms.
  - Applicable framework is a blend of federal and state regulation.
  - Available licences/permits include lottery permits, sports betting licences and promotional permits.
  - Application materials include certified software, software architecture and an SLA between operator and software operator.
  - Lagos State Lotteries and Gaming Authority Law 2021 is described as covering online/retail gaming, computer programming, randomisers, totalisators and internet/mobile/terminal betting services.
  - Remote Operator Permit is described for offshore operators offering online games to Nigerian players.
  - Licensees are treated as DNFBPs for AML purposes and are expected to use a risk-based AML approach.
  - The guide states that Nigerian gambling laws do not provide for use of virtual currencies in gambling.
- Notes: Good legal guide, but not a regulator. Local counsel must verify against current federal/state law and any post-2025 court/regulatory changes.

### NGA-SEC-001 - Securities and Exchange Commission Nigeria, Rules and Regulations repository

- Type: Primary regulator page.
- URL: https://www.sec.gov.ng/our-mandate/regulation/rules-and-regulations/
- Relevance: official SEC repository for digital asset rules and AML/CFT regulations for capital market operators.
- Key evidence:
  - SEC lists "Rules on Issuance, Offering Platforms and Custody of Digital Assets - 11 May 2022".
  - SEC describes the rules as covering DAOPs, Digital Asset Custodians, VASPs and Digital Asset Exchanges.
  - SEC lists capital market operator AML/CFT/CPF regulations dated 12 May 2022.

### NGA-SEC-002 - SEC Nigeria, Rules on Issuance, Offering Platforms and Custody of Digital Assets

- Type: Primary regulator document.
- URL: https://www.sec.gov.ng/documents/8/Rules-on-Issuance-Offering-and-Custody-of-Digital-Assets.pdf
- Relevance: VASP/DAX/DAC/DAOP definitions, digital asset custody, transfer/exchange services, AML/CFT, security, client asset segregation, complaints.
- Key evidence:
  - Rules cover VASPs, Digital Asset Exchanges, Digital Asset Custodians, Digital Asset Offering Platforms and issuance of digital assets.
  - VASP rules apply to platforms facilitating trading, exchange and transfer of virtual assets and persons whose activities involve DLT-related and virtual digital asset services.
  - VASP definition includes exchange between virtual assets and fiat, exchange between virtual assets, transfer of virtual assets, safekeeping/administration of virtual assets and related financial services.
  - "Virtual Asset" is defined as a digital representation of value that can be digitally transferred/traded and used for payment or investment purposes.
  - Technology providers merely providing infrastructure/software to a DAX are excluded from VASP rules.
  - Digital asset custodial rules require segregation/safeguarding of client virtual assets and controls for accurate records.
  - VASP/DAX operators must comply with AML/CFT/PF laws and disclose risk warnings and complaint/dispute processes.
- Notes: These are capital-market digital asset rules, not gambling-specific rules. Application to USDC betting/payouts is an OX-specific legal question.

### NGA-SEC-003 - SEC Nigeria, FinPort Programs: RI and ARIP

- Type: Primary regulator page.
- URL: https://www.sec.gov.ng/fintech-and-innovation-hub-finport/finport-programs-ri-and-arip/
- Relevance: current SEC sandbox paths for fintech/digital assets and VASPs.
- Key evidence:
  - Regulatory Incubation applies to innovative capital-market products.
  - ARIP fast-tracks VASPs and Digital Investment Service Providers involving digital assets, tokenized products and automated financial platforms.
  - ARIP mentions smart-contract governance and AML/CFT compliance as areas of SEC support.
- Notes: It does not say gambling operators may use USDC. It supports the conclusion that digital asset activity may need SEC engagement if OX handles virtual assets for Nigerian users.

### NGA-SEC-004 - SEC Nigeria, Registered FinTech Operators

- Type: Primary regulator page.
- URL: https://www.sec.gov.ng/fintech-and-innovation-hub-finport/registered-fintech-operators/
- Relevance: evidence of SEC-supervised RI/ARIP participants and VASP category.
- Key evidence:
  - Page lists operators sanctioned under SEC sandbox tracks.
  - ARIP participants include digital asset exchanges Busha and Quidax.
  - RI participants include VASP/digital asset tokenization and custody examples.
- Notes: Does not authorize gambling use of virtual assets.

### NGA-SCUML-001 - SCUML / EFCC official website

- Type: Primary AML authority page.
- URL: https://scuml.efcc.gov.ng
- Relevance: DNFBP AML registration, monitoring, reporting and sanctions screening links.
- Key evidence:
  - SCUML supervises, monitors and regulates DNFBPs under Nigeria's AML/CFT regime.
  - SCUML functions include registration/certification of DNFBPs, sensitization, monitoring and supervision.
  - SCUML provides links to UN, Nigeria and OFAC sanctions screening lists.
  - From 1 January 2026, CTR/CBTR reporting is to be submitted through the SCUML portal.

### NGA-NDPC-001 - Nigeria Data Protection Commission official website

- Type: Primary regulator page.
- URL: https://ndpc.gov.ng/
- Relevance: privacy regulator, NDPA 2023, controller/processor registration and data subject rights.
- Key evidence:
  - NDPC states it was established under the Nigeria Data Protection Act 2023.
  - NDPC lists services for data controller/processor registration, breach reporting, audit filing and DPCMI records.
  - NDPC describes data subject rights and controller/processor responsibilities.

### NGA-NDPC-002 - NDPC, Nigeria Data Protection Act GAID 2025

- Type: Primary regulator directive/guidance PDF.
- URL: https://ndpc.gov.ng/wp-content/uploads/2025/07/NDP-ACT-GAID-2025-MARCH-20TH.pdf
- Relevance: territorial scope, controller/processor obligations, cross-border data transfer, DPA, DPIA and emerging technology.
- Key evidence:
  - NDP Act applies to non-Nigerian controllers/processors that process personal data of data subjects in Nigeria or target such data subjects.
  - Article 45 states Part VIII of the NDP Act governs cross-border transfers from Nigeria.
  - Schedule 5 lists cross-border transfer grounds: adequacy decision, Commission-approved transfer instrument, or other lawful bases.
  - Approved transfer instruments may include codes of conduct, certifications, binding corporate rules and standard contractual clauses.
  - GAID includes a data processing agreement requirement and DPIA/technology risk framework.

### NGA-ARCON-001 - Advertising Regulatory Council of Nigeria Act 2022

- Type: Primary statute/gazette copy hosted by Policy and Legal Advocacy Centre.
- URL: https://placng.org/i/wp-content/uploads/2022/10/Advertising-Regulatory-Council-of-Nigeria-Act-2022.pdf
- Relevance: advertising approval, marketing to Nigerian market, foreign advertisers, social responsibility, Nigerian advertising regulator.
- Key evidence:
  - ARCON is established as apex regulatory authority for advertising and marketing communications.
  - The Act applies to advertisements and marketing communications exposed or directed at the Nigerian market.
  - Advertisements must be legal, decent, honest, truthful, respectful and socially responsible.
  - ARCON may regulate foreign-origin corporate entities engaging in advertising directed at Nigeria.
  - Publication/exposure of advertisement targeting Nigeria without prior Standards Panel approval is an offence.

## Sources checked but not relied on as primary

### NGA-QA-001 - PwC Nigeria, Supreme Court verdict on the National Lottery Act

- Type: Secondary law firm/accounting firm alert.
- URL: https://www.pwc.com/ng/en/assets/pdf/supreme-court-verdict-on-the-national-lottery-act.pdf
- Relevance: quality-audit source on post-2024 federal/state regulatory uncertainty.
- Key evidence:
  - PwC describes the 22 November 2024 Supreme Court decision in Suit No. SC/1/2008 as declaring the National Lottery Act invalid.
  - PwC states that the judgment resolves the controversy between Federal Government and State Governments over regulation of lotteries and gaming activities.
  - PwC says operators need to evaluate strategies to meet regulatory regimes in the states where they operate.
- Audit note: useful secondary source, but not the official judgment. It should trigger local counsel review and downgrade of any broad "federal + state regulator" conclusion.

### NGA-QA-002 - Templars, Supreme Court declares National Lottery Act invalid

- Type: Secondary law firm alert.
- URL: https://www.templars-law.com/app/uploads/2024/11/Client-Alert-Supreme-Court-Declares-National-Lottery-Act-Invalid.pdf
- Relevance: quality-audit source on NLRC/National Lottery Act status.
- Key evidence:
  - Templars describes the 22 November 2024 Supreme Court decision in Attorney General of Lagos State v Attorney General of the Federation & Ors as nullifying the National Lottery Act.
  - Templars states that NLRC jurisdiction is now limited to the FCT and states can regulate lottery businesses.
- Audit note: secondary source only. The official judgment and current regulator/state implementation must be obtained before relying on this as final law.

### NGA-SEC-005 - SEC Nigeria, Investments and Securities Act 2025

- Type: Primary statute/regulator-hosted PDF.
- URL: https://sec.gov.ng/documents/1319/Investments_and_Securities_Act_2025_x9rSXtI.pdf
- Relevance: quality-audit source on current digital/virtual asset framework.
- Key evidence:
  - SEC-hosted ISA 2025 material includes virtual assets, digital assets and DLT offers/tokens/products in the investment/securities framework.
- Audit note: the earlier research used SEC 2022 Digital Asset Rules. ISA 2025 and later SEC circulars mean the digital asset analysis should be treated as current-law-sensitive and reviewed by fintech counsel.

### NGA-SEC-006 - SEC Nigeria, Revised Minimum Capital for Regulated Capital Market Entities, Circular No. 26-1, 16 January 2026

- Type: Primary regulator circular.
- URL: https://sec.gov.ng/documents/1427/CIRCULAR_Number_26-1._Minimum_Capital_Requirements.pdf
- Relevance: quality-audit source on current VASP/capital-market operator requirements.
- Key evidence:
  - SEC issued a 2026 circular under ISA 2025 revising minimum capital for regulated capital market entities, including digital-assets-related market segments.
- Audit note: reinforces that the VASP/digital asset regulatory position is not fully captured by the 2022 SEC rules alone.
